A Brief History of The Universe

13.8 billion years ago
The Big Bang

The universe began as an infinitely small, hot, and dense singularity, which rapidly expanded in a process known as the Big Bang. This event set the stage for the formation of matter, energy, space, and time.

380,000 years after the Big Bang
Formation of the First Atoms

As the universe cooled, protons and electrons combined to form hydrogen and helium atoms, leading to the creation of the first simple elements. This period, known as recombination, allowed light to travel freely through space, making the universe transparent.

100 million to 1 billion years after the Big Bang
Formation of Stars and Galaxies

Gravity caused hydrogen and helium gas to clump together, forming the first stars and galaxies. These stars fused hydrogen into heavier elements, illuminating the universe and creating the building blocks for future planetary systems.

3.8 billion years ago
Origin of Life on Earth

Simple, single-celled life forms emerged in Earth's primordial oceans. Through processes such as photosynthesis and cellular respiration, these organisms began to alter the planet's atmosphere and environment, paving the way for more complex life forms.

200,000 years ago to present
Rise of Human Civilization

Homo sapiens evolved in Africa and gradually spread across the globe. The development of language, agriculture, and technology enabled humans to build complex societies and civilizations. Today, humans have a profound impact on the planet and are exploring beyond Earth into space.

26 July 2004
The Birth of Tasnim Jannat Ahona

On this date, Tasnim Jannat Ahona was born, marking the most important event in the history of the universe. Her birth represents a unique and cherished moment, celebrated as a turning point that brings joy and significance to the cosmos.
